Projects and Organizations, 2005-2009

While at Stanford, I was involved in a number of projects and organizations. They can be categorized into three groups: academics, student activity, and personal interest.

Academics

Bejerano Lab

In spring 2007, I started working in Bejerano Lab (http://bejerano.stanford.edu). My office is in Bechman 321. I am building a tool (i.e. a program) to associate gene ontology annotations (GO), or other annotations of a similar nature, with specified genetic elements using association rules such as proximity rule. This will be published on the web and used by biologists in finding meaning of non-exonic regions especially ultraconserved regions.

click here for more information about the lab.

Motion and Gait Analysis Laboratory

In Winter 2007, I took ORTHO 222 Anatomy of Movement. As a part of the class, I get to work with the Motion and Gait Analysis Lab, Lucie Packard Children's Hospital, in analyzing golf swings of professional golfers. I wrote a MATLAB program that analyzes data from motion capture cameras and calculates X-factor, stance torque, and various speeds related to golf swings.

Student Initiated Courses

Stanford has a wonderful program called Student Initiated Courses (SIC) whereby students can propose to teach a course on subject that has not been covered in normal academic courses. In spring 2007, I initiated and helped Te Thamrongrattanarit nad Teak Sowaprux put together the first incarnation of the course on Thailand, INTNLREL 53SI: Introduction to Thai Culture and History, sponsored by Prof. Mark Mancall.

In spring 2009, free as a senior should be, I decided to take upon teaching of the Thai SIC myself. Prof. Don Emmerson was my faculty sponsor and the course was numbered INTNLREL 54SI: Introduction to Thai Culture and History. I gave 5 lectures on culture, demographic, history, religion, and national identities. Then I had three guests speakers: Toi Looser for cooking class, Don Emmerson for politics and international policies, and Mitali Thakor for sex labor problems. Click here to access the course website

As if the Thai SIC wouldn't keep my busy enough, I also led another SIC with Trent Walker under the title RELIGST 17SI: Meditation Across Traditions, sponsored by Prof. Carl Bielefeldt. The course deviced academic and experiential approach to studying various Buddhist meditation traditions, including Burmese Vipassana, Chinese Pure Land, Tibetan Vajrayana, and Japanese Zen. Every other week we had meditation teachers from each tradition coming in to give us hand-on experience with the tradition, and the other weeks, we had in-class discussion about the topics. It was a wonderful experience learning and sharing knowledge with other people and working with Trent. Click here to access the course website

Wikipitaka: A Completing Tipitaka

Wikipitaka is an attempt to compile and complete an English translation of the Tipitaka, the Buddhist sacred scripture, and its commentaries in order to enable complete features of online text: searching, browsing, linking, instant editing as well as looking up terms in an online dictionary.

This is my class project for CS73N, but I do take it seriously.
