Academics & Professional

Genetic Analysis Workshops (GAW17)

GAWs are a collaborative effort among researchers worldwide to evaluate and compare statistical genetic methods relevant to current analytical problems in genetic epidemiology and statistical genetics.

With Kevin Squire, a postdoctoral fellow in Nelson Lab, I join Prof. Berit Kerner and a statistician Tiffany Lin to form a team to participate in GAW17 which will take place in Boston in October. One fun fact is that I will be participating in the workshops under my UCLA affiliation.

We are still in preliminary stage at this point. But we are looking into sequencing quality control issue and how it may affect downstream analyses.

Personal Interest

Shiseikan Kyudo Dojo

In spring 2008, when I studied abroad in Kyoto, I started training Kyudo (Japanese Archery) at Doshisha University. Once returned to Stanford, I pursued my Kyudo training with Yoshiko Buchanan sensei at Shiseikan Dojo in San Jose, following the standard style of All Nippon Kyudo Federation (ANKF).

I passed shodan test in summer 2009 at American Kyudo Seminar in South Carolina.

With the lack of ANKF Kyudo dojo in Boston, I am doing my best to practice Kyudo on my own and get remote instruction from Yoshiko-sensei.

Disneyland Canoe Races

On Saturdays of June and July, I join canoe practices at Newport Aquatic Center. The practices are organized by Disney Store employees participating in Disney's 47th annual Disneyland Canoe Races. The actual races happen in the River of America in Disneyland with the attraction canoes. It is surprising how seriously they are taking this, and it is certainly a substantial exercise. This is a fun morning exercise that always concludes with a yummy brunch by the beach. Sometimes I would go surf at Huntington Beach afterward, certainly living up my LA life here.
