Commute Optimizer

How does your commute change depending on when you leave? This tool charts driving time for a route at every 30-minute departure window across the day, for each weekday. Beneath the duration chart is the STI — Sakata Traffic Index: the rate of change of commute time with respect to departure time, in minutes per minute. An STI of 2 means "each extra minute you wait costs you 2 extra minutes in traffic." A negative STI means waiting actually shortens your drive (though you still arrive later overall).

STI — Sakata Traffic Index
How to read STI:  STI > 0: leaving later adds to your commute time.  STI = 2: Mio's rule — every extra minute costs 2 minutes in traffic.  STI < 0: traffic is clearing fast enough that waiting shortens your drive.  STI ≈ 0: departure time doesn't matter much.

Demo data is modeled on typical Bay Area traffic patterns (I-80, Bay Bridge, US-101) using historical rush-hour curves. For live data based on current conditions, use Custom Analysis below with your own Mapbox token.
